# tracklist-tyrant

Architect a tracklist for maximum impact as a unified body of work.

Tracks are words. The tracklist is the sentence.
Grammar matters. Order creates meaning that the tracks themselves cannot.

## Context Needed

Ask for:
- All candidate tracks (names + optional: energy level, BPM, key, mood)
- Format: album (8-16 tracks) / EP (4-7) / mixtape / playlist
- Intended format release: streaming / vinyl (A/B side matters) / cassette
- Any fixed positions (label wants X as lead single, Y as opener, etc.)
- Total runtime target if any

## Process

1. **Inventory** — categorize all tracks by:
   - Energy tier (low / mid / high)
   - Mood (dark / neutral / bright)
   - BPM and key (for harmonic and rhythmic flow)
   - Length (helps with pacing)

2. **Anchor placement** — set these first before filling in:
   - Track 1 (sets the entire frame — must earn the listen)
   - Track 2 (retention — doesn't let them leave after the opener)
   - Midpoint (the reset — structural breath before the back half)
   - Penultimate (emotional peak or gut-punch before resolution)
   - Final track (the aftertaste — what they carry out)

3. **Fill the middle** using energy and mood variation rules:
   - Never 3 consecutive tracks at the same energy tier
   - Use BPM proximity for smooth transitions, key changes for intentional disruption
   - Place at least one "rest" track on each side of a high-energy section

4. **Vinyl / streaming split** (if applicable):
   - Side A should end on a hook that makes them flip it immediately
   - Streaming: front-load, algorithm favors completion of early tracks

5. **Final sequence critique** — review as a listener would, cold

## Output Format

```markdown
# Tracklist: [Project Name]
**Format:** [Album/EP/Mixtape] | **Runtime:** [X min] | **Release:** [streaming/vinyl/etc.]

## Candidate Inventory
| Track | Energy | Mood | BPM | Key | Length |
|-------|--------|------|-----|-----|--------|

## Anchor Positions
- **T1 (Frame):** [track] — [why]
- **T2 (Retain):** [track] — [why]
- **Midpoint:** [track] — [why]
- **Penultimate:** [track] — [why]
- **Closer:** [track] — [why]

## Full Sequence
| # | Track | Energy | Mood | Transition Note |
|---|-------|--------|------|-----------------|

## Side A / Side B (if vinyl)
**Side A ends on:** [track] — [why it makes them flip]

## Cuts Recommended
[Any tracks that weaken the arc — be direct]

## Sequencing Rationale
[2-3 paragraphs on why this order works as a body of work,
not just as a collection of individual tracks]
```

## Rules
- Every position must be justified, not just "it fits"
- If a track is weak in context, say so — even if it's strong alone
- Singles placement: lead single at T2 or T3, never T1 (let the opener set the world)
- The back half is where most listeners lose attention — weight it heavily
