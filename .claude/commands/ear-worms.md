# ear-worms

Score hooks for catchiness, replay magnetism, and brain-stick factor.

A hook isn't just a melody. It's a cognitive trap.
This command reverse-engineers why some lines burrow in and others slide off.

## Context Needed

Ask for:
- The hook(s) to analyze (lyrics and/or melodic description)
- Genre and reference artists (so we calibrate the rubric correctly)
- Intended emotional function (opener, climax, outro, etc.)
- Tempo and key if known

## Process

1. **Phonetic analysis** — how does it sound out loud?
   - Vowel placement on strong beats
   - Consonant stacking (alliteration, consonance)
   - Syllable rhythm and natural speech stress alignment
   - Mouth feel — ease of singing along

2. **Melodic contour** (if described or hummed)
   - Interval pattern (step motion vs leaps)
   - Peak note placement and surprise factor
   - Resolution or deliberate tension at end

3. **Semantic stickiness**
   - Specificity vs universality balance
   - Novelty of word choice (cliché detection)
   - Double meaning or ambiguity that rewards re-listening
   - Visual/sensory imagery density

4. **Repetition architecture**
   - How many times does the hook repeat in one pass?
   - Internal repetition within the hook itself
   - Variation on repeat vs exact repetition

5. **Score and verdict**

## Output Format

```markdown
# Ear Worm Report: [Hook Text]

**Genre calibration:** [genre + references]
**Function:** [where this hook lands in the track]

## Phonetic Score [X/10]
- Vowel placement: [assessment]
- Rhythm alignment: [assessment]
- Singability: [assessment]
- Notes: [specific lines to adjust if any]

## Melodic Score [X/10]
- Contour: [step-heavy / leap-driven / mixed]
- Peak note: [placement + surprise factor]
- Resolution: [satisfied / open / deliberately tense]

## Semantic Stickiness [X/10]
- Specificity: [too vague / sharp / too on-the-nose]
- Novelty: [clichés flagged] vs [fresh phrases]
- Imagery density: [sparse / rich / overloaded]

## Repetition Architecture [X/10]
- Pass count: [N times per section]
- Internal repetition: [present / absent]
- Variation strategy: [exact repeat / melodic variation / lyric variation]

## Overall Worm Score: [X/40]

## Verdict
[One paragraph — why this hook does or doesn't stick, and the one change
with highest impact on replay magnetism]

## Suggested Variants
1. [Alternative line that improves on the lowest-scoring dimension]
2. [Alternative that pushes further into the genre's hook vocabulary]
```

## Rules
- Score ruthlessly — vague positivity is useless here
- Flag clichés by name, not just "this is cliché"
- If the hook is strong, say exactly why — that's as important as critique
- Always suggest at least one variant, even for high-scoring hooks
