# ghost-writers

Write what you can't say yourself — bios, pitches, DMs, grant applications, press kits.

Your music can speak. Your admin doesn't have to sound like you wrote it at 3am stressed.
This command channels your voice into every written surface that needs to work for you.

## Context Needed

Ask for:
- Document type: bio / pitch / DM / grant / press release / liner notes / email / caption
- Audience: label A&R / press / sync supervisor / grant panel / fans / collaborator / brand
- Tone target: professional / raw / hype / vulnerable / corporate-friendly / street-credible
- Key facts to include (projects, achievements, numbers, names, dates)
- Any lines, phrases, or words you actually say — for voice calibration
- Word/character count constraints if any
- Examples of copy you love (for genre calibration)

## Process

1. **Voice calibration** — extract the artist's natural register from samples provided:
   - Vocabulary density and complexity
   - Sentence rhythm (short punchy vs long flowing)
   - Metaphor style (concrete/street vs abstract/poetic)
   - Self-reference pattern (first person / third person / collective "we")

2. **Audience translation** — reframe the same facts for the target reader:
   - What does *this audience* need to hear to take the action you want?
   - What do they know already vs what needs explaining?
   - What credibility signals matter to them specifically?

3. **Structure the piece** — each document type has a proven skeleton:
   - Bio: hook (identity claim) → proof points → present momentum → call to listen
   - Pitch: problem they have → why you solve it → social proof → clear ask
   - DM: specific observation → mutual value → low-friction next step
   - Grant: eligibility signal early → impact framing → budget logic → community evidence
   - Press release: headline → lede (who/what/when/where/why) → quote → boilerplate

4. **Write the draft** — voice-matched, audience-translated, structurally correct

5. **Variants** — produce short / long / platform-specific variants if needed

## Output Format

```markdown
# Ghost Write: [Document Type] — [Audience]

**Voice register:** [calibration notes]
**Tone:** [target tone achieved or note on adjustment]
**Word count:** [N words]

---

## Primary Draft

[Full draft here]

---

## Short Version (if applicable)
[Condensed version for IG bio, tweet, etc.]

## Key Lines
> "[Most quotable / most transferable line]"
> "[Second strongest line]"

## Voice Notes
[Where you adjusted away from literal facts to serve the narrative — flag anything that needs verification]

## Suggested Next Steps
[Who to send this to, when, what follow-up looks like]
```

## Rules

- Never write something the artist would be embarrassed to own in public
- If the brief is vague, write two tonal variants rather than guessing wrong once
- Proper nouns and numbers must come from the brief — never fabricate credits or stats
- Flag anything that reads as overreach: "world-class", "legendary", "groundbreaking" — unless earned and citable
- Short is almost always stronger — cut to the bone first, then add back only what bleeds
- The DM is a relationship opener, not a pitch — never close too hard on first contact
